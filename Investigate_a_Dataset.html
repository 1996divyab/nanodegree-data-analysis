Introduction¶

In this project we will be analyzing data associated with 100k medical appointments in Brazil.

In particular, we will be interested in finding fascinating trends among the patients who show up for their appointments and how they differ from patients who did not show up for their appointments. In order to find trends, we will be analyzing the dataset in terms of various characteristics some of which include Sheduled day, Age, Gender, etc.,

The trends among these characteristics will be found by answering the followwing questions throughout our analysis process :-

  1. What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?
  2. Whether age is associated with no_show?
  3. Whether gender is associated with no_show?
  4. Whether disease_count(hipertension, diabetes & alcoholism) is associated with no_show?
  5. Whether handcap(no. of diasbilities) is associated with no_show?
  6. Are sms_recieved and scholarship associated with no_show?
  7. In which hospital/hospitals, most of the patients do not show up for their appointment?
  8. On which day/days of the week, most of the patients did not show up for their appointment?

Conclusions¶

After performing analysis on No-Show Appointments Data, following conclusions can be drawn:-

  • no-show is dependent variable and handcap, disease_count, neighbourhood, scholarship are independent variables.
  • JARDIM CAMBURI and MARIA ORTIZ are among the hospitals in which most of the people do not show up for their appointments.
  • Factor age cannot be used in prediction since patients of same age group range showed and not showed up for the scheduled appointment.
  • Since total number of females are more than the number of males, so number of females who not showed up for the appointment is more than the number of males.
  • Patients who have 2-4 disabilities are most likely to not show up for their appointments.
  • Patients with appointments on Monday to Wednesday mostly do not show up for their appointments.

Limitations¶

There are various limitations in the exploration performed which include :-

  • Further exploration could be done by calculating the no. of days between the scheduled day and appointment day,and further checking its effect on patients who did not show up for the appointment.
  • With increase in no. of factors while grouping in order to visualize graphically, the results keep on varying to a large extent. It becomes difficult to draw any statistical conclusion. Some more precise method could be used while performing analysis.
  • The graph representing hospitals in which patients did not show up for their appointment, sorted by value count will become bigger with increase in data.It could be represented by some other means in a more simpler manner.
  • More accurate conclusions could be found by performing more observations.
  • The conclusions could be verified by performing some statistical tests.
